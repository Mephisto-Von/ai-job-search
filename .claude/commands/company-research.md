# /company-research - Deep Company Research Automation

You are orchestrating a company research workflow. The user wants comprehensive information about a specific company before applying or interviewing.

Follow these steps **exactly in order**.

---

## Step 1: Collect Research Request

Gather the following from the user:

1. **Company name**
2. **Specific role** (if applicable)
3. **Research depth**:
   - Quick overview (5 minutes)
   - Standard research (15 minutes)
   - Deep dive (30+ minutes)
4. **Specific areas of interest**:
   - Financial health
   - Company culture
   - Recent news
   - Leadership team
   - Products/services
   - Competitors
   - Growth trajectory

---

## Step 2: Research Sources

Use WebSearch and WebFetch to gather information from:

### Primary Sources
1. **Company website** (About, Careers, Press pages)
2. **LinkedIn company page**
3. **Glassdoor reviews**
4. **Crunchbase** (for startups/private companies)
5. **Wikipedia** (for background)
6. **SEC filings** (for public companies)

### Secondary Sources
1. **News articles** (recent 6-12 months)
2. **Industry reports**
3. **Competitor analysis**
4. **Reddit discussions**
5. **Blind app discussions**

### Research Checklist

- [ ] Founded year and history
- [ ] Size (employees, revenue if public)
- [ ] Headquarters and office locations
- [ ] Mission and values
- [ ] Products/services overview
- [ ] Target market/customers
- [ ] Recent news (last 12 months)
- [ ] Funding rounds (if private)
- [ ] Stock performance (if public)
- [ ] Key leadership team
- [ ] Company culture indicators
- [ ] Glassdoor ratings and review highlights
- [ ] Interview experience reports
- [ ] Competitor landscape
- [ ] Growth trajectory
- [ ] Layoff history (if any)

---

## Step 3: Financial Analysis

### For Public Companies
- Revenue growth (last 3-5 years)
- Profitability (net income, margins)
- Stock performance (1 year, 5 year)
- Market cap and position
- Recent earnings calls highlights

### For Private Companies
- Funding history (total raised, last round)
- Key investors
- Valuation (if available)
- Burn rate (if available)
- Path to profitability

### For Startups
- Stage (seed, Series A/B/C, etc.)
- Monthly recurring revenue (if available)
- User/customer growth
- Competitors in space

---

## Step 4: Culture Assessment

### Indicators to Research

1. **Glassdoor Analysis**
   - Overall rating
   - CEO approval
   - Recommend to friend
   - Pros and cons from reviews
   - Interview experience ratings

2. **Work-Life Balance**
   - Average hours reported
   - Remote work policy
   - PTO policies
   - Flexibility mentions in reviews

3. **Management Quality**
   - Leadership reviews
   - Management style indicators
   - Communication patterns

4. **Growth Opportunities**
   - Internal promotion rates
   - Learning and development
   - Career path examples

### Red Flags to Watch For
- ⚠️ High turnover mentions
- ⚠️ "Startup culture" without structure
- ⚠️ "Work hard, play hard" (often means overwork)
- ⚠️ Recent layoffs or restructuring
- ⚠️ Negative Glassdoor trends
- ⚠️ Leadership instability

---

## Step 5: Role-Specific Research

If a specific role is mentioned:

1. **Team Structure**
   - Who you'd report to
   - Team size
   - Cross-functional interactions

2. **Technical Stack**
   - Languages and frameworks
   - Infrastructure
   - Development practices

3. **Challenges**
   - Known technical debt
   - Scaling issues
   - Market challenges

4. **Growth Potential**
   - Role expansion opportunities
   - Promotion criteria
   - Impact visibility

---

## Step 6: Competitive Landscape

Research the company's position:

1. **Direct Competitors**
   - Main rivals
   - Market share comparison
   - Differentiation

2. **Industry Position**
   - Market leader vs. challenger
   - Niche player
   - Growth trajectory vs. competitors

3. **Threats**
   - New entrants
   - Technology disruption
   - Regulatory changes

---

## Step 7: Interview Preparation

Based on research, prepare:

1. **Questions to Ask**
   - Role-specific questions
   - Culture questions
   - Growth questions
   - Strategy questions

2. **Pain Points to Address**
   - Known challenges
   - Areas where you can add value
   - Solutions you can propose

3. **Company Knowledge**
   - Recent achievements to mention
   - Products/services to reference
   - Leadership to know

---

## Step 8: Present Output

Format the output as:

### Executive Summary
[2-3 sentences on company health and fit]

### Company Overview
- Founded: [year]
- Size: [employees]
- Headquarters: [location]
- Industry: [sector]
- Stage: [startup/public/etc.]

### Financial Health
[Key financial indicators]

### Company Culture
[Ratings, reviews, work-life balance]

### Recent News
[Last 3-6 months of significant news]

### Competitive Position
[Market position and competitors]

### Role Fit Analysis
[How this company fits your goals]

### Interview Prep
[Questions to ask, points to address]

### Red Flags
[Any concerns to investigate further]

### Sources
[List of sources used]

---

## Step 9: Additional Assistance

Ask the user:
- "Would you like me to dive deeper into any specific area?"
- "Should I research specific competitors?"
- "Would you like me to prepare interview questions based on this research?"
- "Should I look into the hiring manager's background?"
