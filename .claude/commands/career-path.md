## Step 4: Career Path Options

### Option A: Vertical Progression (Traditional)

```
Current Role → Next Level → Senior → Lead → Manager → Director → VP
```

**Pros:**
- Clear progression
- Higher compensation
- More responsibility
- Better title

**Cons:**
- May require specific company/industry
- Longer time horizon
- More competition
- Less flexibility

### Option B: Lateral Move (Breadth)

```
Current Role → Similar Role (Different Industry) → Specialized Role → Expert
```

**Pros:**
- Broader experience
- New challenges
- Different perspective
- More flexibility

**Cons:**
- May not increase title
- Requires learning new domain
- May not increase salary immediately
- Harder to track progression

### Option C: Technical Specialist (Depth)

```
Current Role → Senior Specialist → Principal → Distinguished → Fellow
```

**Pros:**
- Deep expertise
- High demand
- Less management responsibility
- Consulting opportunities

**Cons:**
- Limited to technical roles
- May plateau in compensation
- Requires continuous learning
- Less business exposure

### Option D: Leadership Track

```
Current Role → Team Lead → Manager → Director → VP → C-Suite
```

**Pros:**
- Higher compensation
- Strategic impact
- Building teams
- Business influence

**Cons:**
- Less hands-on work
- More politics
- Higher stress
- Requires different skills

### Option E: Entrepreneurial Path

```
Current Role → Side Project → Startup → Founder → CEO
```

**Pros:**
- Full control
- Unlimited upside
- Creative freedom
- Building something

**Cons:**
- High risk
- Uncertain income
- Long hours
- No safety net

### Option F: Portfolio Career

```
Current Role → Part-time + Consulting + Teaching + Side Projects
```

**Pros:**
- Diverse income
- Flexibility
- Multiple interests
- Risk mitigation

**Cons:**
- Less focus
- Complex to manage
- May lack depth
- No single career identity
