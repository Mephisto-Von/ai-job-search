# /referral - Find Connections at Target Companies

You are orchestrating a referral finder workflow. The user wants to find connections at a specific company to get a referral or insider information.

Follow these steps **exactly in order**.

---

## Step 1: Collect Target Company Information

Gather the following from the user:

1. **Target company name**
2. **Role/position** (if specific role)
3. **Department/team** (if known)
4. **Your connection sources**:
   - LinkedIn connections
   - Alumni network
   - Previous colleagues
   - Personal contacts
   - Professional associations

---

## Step 2: Research the Company

Before finding connections, research:

1. **Company overview**:
   - Size, structure, locations
   - Recent news or developments
   - Company culture and values

2. **Department structure**:
   - Engineering, product, design teams
   - Reporting structure (if public)
   - Key leaders

3. **Referral process**:
   - Does the company have a formal referral program?
   - What are the typical referral incentives?
   - Any specific referral requirements?

---

## Step 3: Connection Mapping Strategy

### Level 1: Direct Connections
- People you know personally
- Former colleagues at the company
- Alumni from your school

### Level 2: 2nd Degree Connections
- People connected to your direct connections
- Mutual connections who can introduce you

### Level 3: Weak Ties
- People in similar roles at other companies
- Industry contacts
- Professional association members

---

## Step 4: Outreach Template Creation

Create personalized outreach messages for different scenarios:

### Scenario A: Direct Connection (Someone You Know)

**Subject:** Quick question about [Company]

**Message:**
Hi [Name],

Hope you're doing well! I noticed you're working at [Company] and I'm really interested in the [Role] position there. I was wondering if you'd be open to chatting about your experience or potentially referring me?

I've attached my resume for context. Would love to catch up either way!

Best,
[Your Name]

---

### Scenario B: 2nd Degree Connection (Mutual Friend)

**Subject:** Introduction via [Mutual Connection]

**Message:**
Hi [Name],

[Mutual Connection] suggested I reach out - I'm really interested in the [Role] position at [Company] and they mentioned you might be a great person to connect with about the role.

Would you have 15 minutes for a quick chat about your experience at [Company]? I'd love to learn more about the team and culture.

Thanks so much!

[Your Name]

---

### Scenario C: Alumni Connection

**Subject:** Fellow [University] Alum - [Company] Question

**Message:**
Hi [Name],

Fellow [University] alum here! I saw you're working at [Company] and I'm really interested in the [Role] position. I was hoping you might be open to connecting about your experience there.

I've been following [Company]'s work in [specific area] and would love to learn more about the team culture and growth opportunities.

Would you be available for a quick chat?

Best,

---

### Scenario D: Cold Outreach (No Connection)

**Subject:** Interested in [Role] at [Company]

**Message:**
Hi [Name],

I came across your profile and was really impressed by your work at [Company] - especially [specific project or achievement].

I'm currently exploring opportunities in [field] and the [Role] position at [Company] caught my attention. I'd love to learn more about the team and potentially discuss how my experience in [your expertise] might be a good fit.

Would you have 15-20 minutes for a virtual coffee chat?

Thanks for considering!

[Your Name]

---

## Step 5: Research Questions for Connections

When you connect with someone, ask:

1. **About the role:**
   - What does a typical day look like?
   - What are the biggest challenges?
   - What's the team structure?

2. **About the company:**
   - How would you describe the culture?
   - What's the growth trajectory?
   - How has the company changed since you joined?

3. **About the hiring process:**
   - What does the interview process look like?
   - What makes a candidate stand out?
   - Any tips for applying?

4. **About the referral:**
   - Would you be comfortable referring me?
   - Is there anything specific I should mention?
   - What's the referral process like?

---

## Step 6: Referral Request Strategy

### Timing
- Wait until you've had a conversation before asking for a referral
- Don't ask in the first message
- Build rapport first

### How to Ask
- Be direct but polite
- Explain why you're a good fit
- Make it easy for them (attach resume, link to application)

### After Getting a Referral
- Send a thank you note
- Keep them updated on your progress
- Offer to help them in return
